## 1. SINGLE_FAMILY_HOME

**Footprint:** Rectangle, L-shape, or T-shape — typically 40–60 ft wide × 30–50 ft deep
**Wall thickness:** 6" exterior (double-line), 4" interior partitions

**Typical Room Layout:**
- Entry/foyer near front-center → great room
- Open kitchen-dining-living zone as core
- Master suite at rear (split from secondary bedrooms)
- 2 secondary bedrooms sharing hall bath
- Attached garage: 20×20 to 24×24 ft
- Laundry off kitchen or garage hallway

**Standard Sizes:**
- 3 bed / 2 bath: 1,200–2,400 sq ft
- Bedroom: 10×12 to 14×16 ft
- Master suite: 14×16 + 8×10 bath + 6×8 closet
- Kitchen: 12×14, open to 10×12 dining
- Great room: 16×20 to 20×24

---

## 2. CONTAINER_HOME

**Footprint:** One or more 8 ft wide × 20 or 40 ft long rectangles
**Wall thickness:** Thin steel walls (~3–4" equivalent)

**Container modules:**
- 20 ft container: 8×20 exterior, ~7.5 ft interior width, 160 sq ft
- 40 ft container: 8×40 exterior, ~7.5 ft interior width, 320 sq ft

**Common configurations:**
- Single container (linear): 1 bed studio
- Side-by-side two 40 ft: ~16 ft wide × 40 ft = ~640 sq ft
- L-shape (two containers at 90°)
- Stacked (2-story)

**Typical Room Layout (2 containers side-by-side):**
- Container 1: Open kitchen + living/dining + bathroom
- Container 2: Master bedroom + 2nd bedroom/office
- Plumbing concentrated at one end

**Standard Sizes:**
- Studio (1×20): 160 sq ft
- 1 bed (1×40): 320 sq ft
- 2 bed (2×40 side-by-side): 640 sq ft
- 3–4 bed (3–4 containers): 960–1,280 sq ft

---

## 3. BARNDOMINIUM

**Footprint:** Large rectangle — 30×50, 40×60, 50×80, or 60×100 ft common
**Ceiling:** Shop side 14–18 ft; living side 8–10 ft

**Typical Layout (40×60):**
- Shop/garage half: 40×30 ft, 2–3 overhead roll-up doors on gable end
- Living half: 40×30 ft with 2–3 bed, 2 bath, kitchen, great room, laundry
- Covered porch on front of living side
- Mudroom bridges living and shop zones

**Key Drawing Features:**
- Heavy exterior steel frame lines
- Roll-up doors shown as thick dashed lines across gable
- Living side has standard residential room partitions
- Wraparound or partial porch shown as dashed roof overhang

---

## 4. BARN

**Footprint:** Rectangle, typically 24–60 ft wide × 30–120 ft long
**Structure:** Post-and-beam bents repeat every 8–12 ft

**Typical Layout (30×60):**
- Center aisle: 10–12 ft wide, runs full length
- Horse stalls: 10×12 or 12×12 each, 5 per side
- Large sliding doors at both gable ends (12–16 ft wide)
- Tack room (10×12) + feed room (10×12) + wash stall (12×12) at one end
- Hayloft above full footprint — accessed by interior stair/ladder

**Common Sizes:**
- 24×24: 2 stalls + loft
- 30×40: 4–6 stalls + tack room + loft
- 36×60+: 8–12 stalls + support rooms + loft

---

## 5. LOG_CABIN

**Footprint:** Compact rectangle — 16×24 to 24×40 ft typical
**Wall thickness:** 8–12 inches (notched log construction — drawn with hatching between double lines)

**Key Features:**
- Corner notches visible at wall intersections
- Stone fireplace centered on end wall or interior wall (wide wall mass in plan)
- Full-width covered front porch, 6–8 ft deep
- Sleeping loft accessed by open staircase (very common)
- Low overhanging roofline

**Typical Room Layout (24×40):**
- Entry from front porch → great room (living + dining + kitchen)
- 1–2 bedrooms or sleeping loft
- 1 full bathroom
- Optional rear deck

**Standard Sizes:**
- Small: 16×24 (384 sq ft) — open studio + loft
- Medium: 24×32 (768 sq ft) — 2 bed, 1 bath
- Comfortable: 24×40 (960 sq ft) — 2–3 bed, 1–2 bath

---

## 6. A_FRAME

**Footprint:** Narrow rectangle — 20–28 ft wide × 24–40 ft deep
**Key constraint:** Loft level is much narrower than main floor (only central portion usable)

**Typical Room Layout:**
- Main floor: full-width open living/kitchen/dining + bathroom + optional ground bedroom at rear
- Loft: sleeping area 10–14 ft wide (head clearance zone only)
- Front deck/porch on gable face
- Open-riser staircase to loft, centrally placed
- Large triangular glass gable walls front and rear

**Drawing Notes:**
- Loft shown as dashed partial rectangle inside main outline
- Roof lines come all the way to grade (or near it) — no vertical exterior walls on sides
- Sloped ceiling zone shown with hatching on loft plan

**Standard Sizes:**
- Small: 20×24 to 24×32 (480–768 sq ft main floor)
- Medium: 24×36 to 28×40 (864–1,120 sq ft total with loft)

---

## 7. SHED

**Footprint:** Simple rectangle — 6×8 to 12×20 ft
**No plumbing, no HVAC, no foundation**

**Key Features:**
- Double doors on gable end (most common) for equipment access
- 1–2 windows on side walls
- Interior: single open room or simple U-shaped shelving layout
- Roof overhang shown as dashed line outside wall lines

**Standard Sizes:**
- Small: 6×8, 8×8 (48–64 sq ft)
- Medium: 8×12, 10×12 (96–120 sq ft)
- Large: 10×16, 12×20 (160–240 sq ft)

---

## 8. WORKSHOP

**Footprint:** Rectangle — 12×20 to 30×40 ft
**Purpose:** Tools, equipment, projects

**Key Features:**
- Wide entry doors or overhead door on gable wall
- Workbench: 2 ft deep rectangles along 1–3 walls
- Central open floor area for large equipment (60–70% of space)
- Optional: small bathroom (8×8) and office nook in corner
- Windows on side walls for natural light

**Standard Sizes:**
- Minimal: 12×20 (240 sq ft)
- Common: 16×24 to 20×24 (384–480 sq ft)
- Full shop: 24×36 to 30×40 (864–1,200 sq ft)

---

## 9. GARAGE

**Footprint:** Rectangle driven by number of car bays
**Key feature:** Overhead door(s) across front wall

**Bay Sizing:**
- 1-car: 12–14 ft wide × 20–24 ft deep
- 2-car: 20–24 ft wide × 20–24 ft deep
- 3-car: 30–36 ft wide × 22–24 ft deep

**Drawing Notes:**
- Overhead door: double parallel lines across front wall with hidden-line hatching
- Single entry door on side or rear wall
- Optional workbench (2 ft deep) along rear wall
- Optional loft storage shown as dashed rectangle with staircase

**Standard Door Sizes:**
- Single overhead: 9–10 ft wide × 7–8 ft tall
- Double overhead: 16 ft wide × 7–8 ft tall

---

## 10. TINY_HOME

**Footprint:** Very narrow rectangle
- THOW (Tiny House on Wheels): strictly 8 ft wide × 20–32 ft long
- Foundation tiny home: 16–20 ft wide × 20–24 ft long

**Key Features:**
- Sleeping loft at one or both ends (shown as dashed rectangle)
- Steep stair-step bookcase or ship's ladder to loft
- Bathroom: 4×6 or 4×8 at one end
- Galley kitchen: 2 ft deep counter along one wall
- Every square inch used — multi-function furniture zones

**Typical Layout (8×24 THOW):**
- Rear: sleeping loft above bathroom
- Middle: bathroom + galley kitchen
- Front: living area (sofa/pull-out bed)

**Standard Sizes:**
- 8×20: ~148 sq ft main + ~65 sq ft loft
- 8×24: ~179 sq ft main + ~84 sq ft loft
- Foundation: 16×20 to 20×24 = 320–480 sq ft

---

## 11. DOME_HOME

**Footprint:** CIRCLE (or intersecting circles for multi-room)
**Types:** Geodesic dome or monolithic concrete dome

**Diameter Guide:**
- 20 ft: ~314 sq ft
- 30 ft: ~707 sq ft
- 36 ft: ~1,017 sq ft
- 40 ft: ~1,257 sq ft
- 50 ft: ~1,963 sq ft

**Key Features:**
- Floor plan is a true circle — all walls are arcs or radiate from center
- Rooms arranged like pie slices around a central open great room
- Entry vestibule at perimeter
- Optional loft: partial circle mezzanine ring
- Double dome (2 circles joined): figure-8 plan shape

**Typical Layout (36 ft diameter):**
- Center: open living/dining/kitchen
- Perimeter: 3 pie-wedge bedrooms + 2 bathrooms
- Optional loft above center

---

## 12. QUONSET_HUT

**Footprint:** Long rectangle (plan view) — semicircular arch in section
**Models:** Q-model (arch to grade), S-model (vertical sidewalls + arch), A-model (angular)

**Common Sizes:**
- 20×40 ft (800 sq ft)
- 30×60 ft (1,800 sq ft)
- 40×80 ft (3,200 sq ft)

**Key Features:**
- Floor plan is a rectangle; arch ceiling is the defining feature in section
- Entry doors on flat gable endwalls
- Center of plan = tallest zone (ideal for living/kitchen)
- Side zones = lower ceiling — used for bedrooms/baths
- Windows cut into arch sides or gable ends
- Loft possible in S-model (uses vertical sidewall height)

**Typical Layout (30×60):**
- Front gable entry → living/dining
- Center open: kitchen + living (full arch height)
- Sides and rear: 2–3 bedrooms + 1–2 baths (lower ceiling zones)

---

## 13. SILO

**Footprint:** CIRCLE per floor level (stacked discs)
**Typical diameter:** 18–30 ft for converted grain silos; up to 40 ft purpose-built

**Area by Diameter:**
- 18 ft: ~254 sq ft/floor
- 21 ft: ~346 sq ft/floor
- 24 ft: ~452 sq ft/floor
- 28 ft: ~615 sq ft/floor

**Key Features:**
- Every floor plan is a circle
- No right-angle rooms — all walls are arcs
- Rooms carved as quadrant pie slices
- Entry door cut into base of cylindrical wall
- Interior staircase (straight or spiral) against curved wall
- Two silos side-by-side: figure-8 or Venn diagram plan shape

**Typical Layout (24 ft diameter, 2-story):**
- Level 1: Entry + kitchen (quarter) + bathroom (quarter) + living (half)
- Level 2: Bedroom (full circle)

---

## 14. POLE_BARN

**Footprint:** Large open rectangle — 24×30 to 60×100 ft
**Structure:** Posts at perimeter every 8–12 ft, no interior structural walls

**Key Features:**
- Posts shown as solid squares around perimeter at regular intervals
- Interior is completely open (clear span)
- Large sliding or overhead doors on gable ends
- Optional lean-to addition: narrower rectangle attached to long side
- Optional corner rooms: small office/bathroom/storage partition in one corner
- No perimeter foundation — just post footings shown as circles

**Common Sizes:**
- Small: 24×30 to 30×40
- Medium: 36×60 to 40×60
- Large: 50×80 to 60×100

---

## 15. EARTHSHIP

**Footprint:** U-shape / horseshoe
**Orientation:** Always south-facing (passive solar design required)

**Key Features:**
- North wall: buried in earth berm — extremely thick (24–36 inches), no windows
- South face: continuous greenhouse glazing wall, 6–10 ft deep × full width
- Two "wings" of rooms (east and west) connected by greenhouse corridor
- All living spaces radiate off greenhouse corridor
- Cisterns/water tanks in north berm zone
- Raised planting beds along south glazing (24–30" deep)
- Room depth max: 18–24 ft (thermal mass constraint)

**Typical Layout (3-bedroom):**
- South: greenhouse corridor (full width, 6–8 ft deep)
- West wing: master bedroom + bathroom
- East wing: 2 bedrooms + bathroom
- Center: kitchen + living/dining + utility

**Standard Sizes:**
- Studio: ~600 sq ft
- 2 bed: ~1,200 sq ft
- 3 bed: ~1,800–2,000 sq ft

---

## 16. PASSIVE_SOLAR

**Footprint:** Elongated rectangle — longer E-W axis than N-S depth
**Orientation:** Long axis runs east-west; primary glazing faces true south

**Key Features:**
- South wall: heavily glazed (large windows = 5–10% of floor area)
- North wall: few or no windows — solid, garage or storage backing it
- Compact room depth: ≤18 ft for effective solar radiant function
- Overhangs: dashed lines extending 2–3 ft beyond south wall
- Optional sunspace/solarium attached to south face
- Thermal mass zones: concrete slab or masonry tiles in sun-exposed rooms

**Room Placement (south to north):**
- South: living room + dining room + sunroom/solarium
- Center: kitchen + primary bedroom
- North: garage + utility + storage + secondary bathrooms

**Standard Sizes:**
- 1,000–2,500 sq ft typical
- E-W dimension: 50–70 ft
- N-S dimension: 24–36 ft (shallow = thermally effective)
- South glazing: 80–150 sq ft for 1,500 sq ft home
